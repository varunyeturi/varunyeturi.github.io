About

I've always been more interested in understanding things than just describing them.

A lot of my work starts with information that feels disconnected, overly technical, difficult to explain, or buried under layers of jargon. My role is usually figuring out how to bring those pieces together in a way that feels coherent, readable, and meaningful to the people on the other side of it.

Some projects require structure and precision. Others need personality, emotional awareness, or narrative flow. I enjoy the challenge of adapting communication to different audiences and goals—whether that means simplifying a technical concept, shaping executive messaging, writing thought leadership, or building content that feels more human and engaging.

Over time, I've also developed a strong interest in AI-assisted workflows and prompt design. I use tools like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity as part of my research, ideation, and refinement process—not to replace thinking, but to expand it.

More than anything, I care about making ideas feel connected. When communication is done well, people don't have to work as hard to understand each other.

Executive Outbound From: Senior Sales Executive, Innova  ·  To: Tier 1 Carrier Executive
Subject
A practical path to cost transformation in 2026

Hello,

I've been reflecting on our work together supporting [Tier 1 carrier]'s revenue growth in 2025 and where it makes sense to focus as we look ahead to 2026.

As we think about the year ahead, we've aligned our efforts around four priorities—Radical Cost Transformation, Legacy Modernization, Revenue Improvement, and AI Enablement & Adoption. While all four are important, cost feels like the most immediate lever, given how directly it influences everything else.

From what we've seen at this scale, reaching a multi-billion-dollar run-rate reduction usually doesn't come from rate discussions alone. The bigger gains tend to come from changing how work is structured and delivered day to day.

In practice, that thinking has centered on a few concrete shifts:

Global Delivery (ODC): The approach would start by shifting an agreed set of workloads into Innova's lower-cost delivery centers, including Hyderabad, focusing first on areas where cost relief can be realized quickly without disrupting existing teams or outcomes.

Build-Operate-Transfer (BOT): Delivery would first be established and stabilized under Innova's management, with the right governance, processes, and talent in place. Once the model is operating predictably, ownership would transition to [the client], providing long-term control while minimizing transition risk upfront.

Vendor Consolidation: We would take on the coordination and delivery responsibility for selected tail-spend vendors by bringing that work under Innova's managed model, easing the operational burden on [the client] while tightening consistency and control across those engagements.

The next step would be to work with your team to identify a set of initiatives that can realistically deliver $2–3M in annual savings, and then work through them together.

If it would be helpful, I'd be glad to walk through this thinking and hear how it aligns with your current priorities. Would you have 15 minutes next week to talk it through?

Best regards
Executive Outbound From: Senior Sales Executive, Innova  ·  To: Tier 1 Carrier Executive
Subject
AI enablement and execution in 2026

Hello,

Following our support of [Tier 1 carrier]'s revenue growth in 2024, I've been thinking about where AI enablement realistically creates momentum as we look ahead to 2026.

That's where Innova has concentrated its efforts—applying platform-agnostic AI capabilities in places where they can actually be put to work. A few examples that may be relevant:

Agentic AI for Sales: Our teams built sales coaching agents that sit directly in the flow of a seller's day, using real-time context rather than static rules. That approach led to a measurable productivity lift of more than 30%, without requiring sellers to change how they work.

Automated Lead Recovery: In one engagement, Agentic AI was used to re-evaluate leads that had already been marked as “negative.” By reassessing intent and context, the work uncovered tens of thousands of viable leads from a much larger pool that would otherwise have been ignored.

AI-Enabled QA: Rather than layering AI on top of existing QA processes, our teams embedded AI-driven testing directly into the pipeline. That shift reduced manual effort and increased QA productivity by around 40%, while also improving consistency.

Legacy Code Modernization: We applied AI to modernize more than one million lines of legacy code, accelerating modernization timelines and reducing required analyst involvement by roughly 50%.

Data Readiness: Data quality is often the quiet constraint in AI programs, so we built automated pipelines that continuously feed LLMs with clean, customer-relevant data and keep models grounded in operational reality.

If that aligns with what you're seeing, I'd be interested in hearing how AI is landing in your environment today and where execution has been harder than expected.

Best regards

Award Nomination

Professional recognition, written personally.

The Women in Technology (WIT) Awards recognize leaders whose work, influence, and personal impact extend beyond their role or industry title. This was the initial nomination I wrote for Akkshada Maniyan, and while a later submission ultimately helped move her into finalist status, this version was the one that first put her on people's radar and generated a strong response internally.

From the beginning, I wanted the writing to feel human first—centered around perseverance, leadership, resilience, and the experiences that shaped who she became. Instead of treating the nomination like a list of accomplishments, the focus stayed on telling a story people could emotionally connect with and remember afterward.

This ended up being one of my favorite assignments I've worked on because it gave me the opportunity to blend professional writing with narrative storytelling in a much more personal way. I genuinely enjoy work like this—finding the human thread inside someone's experience and shaping it into something that feels meaningful, memorable, and authentic to who they are.

Reproduced below as a professional writing sample.
Why this nominee should be recognized as Woman of the Year

Fifteen years ago, Akkshada Maniyan faced an unimaginable heartbreak. Her sister, trapped in a brutal cycle of domestic violence, could no longer care for her children. With a heart overflowing with love and courage, Akkshada adopted her sister's kids, raising them as her own.

As a single mother in the male-dominated tech industry, she faced immense challenges. Yet, with every setback, she found the strength to persevere. Her ascent to VP of Learning & Organizational Development at Innova Solutions is not just a career milestone; it's a testament to her indomitable will.

Akkshada's groundbreaking Innova Instagram Learning program became a lifeline for women, particularly single mothers, who were juggling work, family, and ambition. She understood their struggles clearly; she had lived them.

Her story is a reminder that even in the face of adversity, the human spirit can rise above, transforming pain into a powerful force for change.

Additional context on the nominee

Akkshada's influence extends far beyond her professional achievements. She is a prolific author, sought-after speaker, and a passionate advocate for women in STEAM. Her book, “The Serial Influencer,” has become a bestseller, offering professionals the tools to forge meaningful connections.

Yet, it is Akkshada's dedication to empowering others that truly defines her legacy. Akkshada is committed to aiding women and children who are victims of domestic abuse. In her work with Gentle Awakening and ExecutiveCoaching.Org, she emphasizes career guidance, volunteering over 2,000 hours of pro-bono coaching. This support is crucial, as it enables them to achieve financial independence and reduces the likelihood of returning to abusive environments.

By fostering a supportive community, Akkshada is making a profound impact, inspiring future generations to chase their dreams with fearlessness. Her compassion creates a ripple effect, touching hearts and changing lives—one person at a time.

Short bio

Akkshada Maniyan is the VP of L&OD at Innova Solutions, where she develops learning programs for a global audience of more than 20,000 employees. Known for her engaging presence, she has spoken to 10,000+ women at various webcasts, panels, and online courses via MasterClass—emboldening them to navigate and excel in a male-dominated field. Akkshada is a LinkedIn Top Voice and part of the AskMe Network, where she conducts personalized coaching sessions. Her work extends beyond typical professional roles, focusing on creating equitable opportunities and fostering a culture of inclusion.
